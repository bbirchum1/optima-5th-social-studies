Part 2 Dispatch from the Field — Primary Source Analysis
Primary Source — Valley Forge, c. 1778
George Washington, letter to his troops at Valley Forge, c. 1778
"To see men without clothes to cover their nakedness, without blankets to lie on, without shoes — and submitting to it without a murmur is a proof of patience and obedience which in my opinion can scarce be paralleled."
A
What does this source tell us about the conditions at Valley Forge? Give TWO specific details from the text.
B
Washington says his men showed "patience and obedience." What does this tell us about what he valued as a leader? What virtue is he describing?
C
This source was written by Washington himself. What perspective might be missing from this account of Valley Forge? Who else might have described the experience differently?
Part 3 Fact-Check the Hasty Herald — Extended Response
⚡ Hasty Herald — Breaking Report
"America won the Revolutionary War because American soldiers were brave and patriotic — that's all it took."
The Hasty Herald has oversimplified the story! Make a claim about the MULTIPLE factors that actually led to American victory. Include at least THREE specific factors (military, diplomatic, economic, or geographic) with evidence to support each one.
Use this rubric to check your own work before submitting.
LevelWhat it looks like
SecureNames at least THREE specific factors (e.g., French alliance, geography/terrain, British supply problems, Valley Forge training) with evidence for each. Goes well beyond "they were brave."
DevelopingNames TWO specific factors with some evidence. May mention bravery as one factor but adds at least one concrete, specific factor.
BeginningSays "they were brave" or "they wanted freedom" without naming specific military, diplomatic, or geographic factors.

Part 4 Virtue in Action — Personal Reflection
Perseverance
Valley Forge is one of the most powerful examples of perseverance in American history. Soldiers stayed through brutal cold and hunger, training and rebuilding rather than giving up.
Think about a time in your own learning this year that required perseverance — a difficult lesson, a hard question, or a concept that took a while to understand. Connect your personal experience to what the Continental Army showed at Valley Forge.
