Part 2 Source Task
Primary Source · Eyewitness Account
Adapted from the account of Bernal Díaz del Castillo, Spanish soldier, describing Tenochtitlan — Aztec capital, c. 1519
"When we saw so many cities and villages built in the water, and other great towns on dry land, and that straight and level causeway going towards the great city, we were amazed. It was like the enchantments they tell of in books. And some of our soldiers even asked whether the things that we saw were not a dream. I do not know how to describe it, seeing things as we did that had never been heard of or seen before, and not even dreamed about."

Read the source carefully, then answer each question using evidence from the text.

A
Is this a primary or secondary source? Give TWO pieces of evidence from the source itself to support your answer.
B
What does this source tell us about the Aztec civilization? Write one specific detail you can observe directly from the text and one inference you can make about the Aztec people.
C
This source was written by a Spanish soldier who came to conquer the Aztec. What perspective does this author represent? What important information about the Aztec might this source leave out?
Part 3 Civilizations Comparison Claim

The Hasty Herald claims: "All ancient American civilizations were basically the same — just people building pyramids and living in the jungle." Choose TWO of the civilizations you studied in Unit 3. Make a claim about the most important way these two civilizations were similar OR different. Use specific evidence from each civilization to support your claim, and explain your reasoning. Do not just list facts — connect the evidence to your argument.

Aim for at least 4 sentences: claim → evidence from each civilization → reasoning

Part 4 Virtue Lens Reflection
Courtesy

Courtesy means treating people with respect — including people from the past whose descendants are still alive today. When historians study the Maya, Aztec, Inca, Mound Builders, or Ancestral Pueblo, they must use accurate names, tell complete stories, and avoid calling ancient people "primitive" or "simple." What does courtesy look like when studying ancient civilizations? Give one specific example of how a courteous historian would approach this topic differently from someone who is careless or disrespectful.
