Part 2 Source Task
Two Sources · Examine Both Carefully
Source A — Spanish Explorer's Account, 1519

When we arrived at the great city, I could not speak, for I had never seen anything so magnificent. The streets were wide, and canals ran alongside them. Markets were everywhere, filled with gold, cloth, and food of every kind. It was larger than any city in Spain.

Source B — American Textbook, 1952

Before Columbus arrived, North America was a vast, mostly empty wilderness. A few small tribes of hunters and gatherers roamed the land.

Question 2A

Which source gives more reliable evidence about the scale and complexity of pre-Columbian civilizations? Explain your reasoning — what specific evidence does that source provide?

Question 2B

What important information does Source B get WRONG or leave out? Use specific evidence from Units 3 and 4 to explain what a more accurate account would include.

Question 2C

A historian needs to use sources carefully. What questions should an honest editor ask about Source A before using it? Think about whose perspective it represents and what it might leave out.
