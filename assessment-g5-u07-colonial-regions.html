Part 2 Source Task
Primary Source · The Interesting Narrative of Olaudah Equiano
Adapted from the memoir of Olaudah Equiano, formerly enslaved, c. 1789 — describing his experience aboard a slave ship during the Middle Passage
"I was soon put down under the decks, and there I received such a salutation in my nostrils as I had never experienced in my life: so that, with the loathsomeness of the stench, and crying together, I became so sick and low that I was not able to eat, nor had I the least desire to taste anything. I now wished for the last friend, death, to relieve me."

Read the source carefully, then answer each question using evidence from the text and your knowledge of colonial history.

A
What does this source tell us about the experience of enslaved Africans during the Middle Passage? Give two specific details from the text to support your answer.
B
This source was written as a memoir long after the events described. Does that make it less reliable as historical evidence? Explain your reasoning.
C
Whose perspective is not represented in most colonial-era historical records? Why is it important for historians to seek out sources like Equiano's memoir?
Part 3 Colonial Life Claim

The Hasty Herald claims: "Life in the thirteen colonies was the same for everyone — hardworking settlers building a new land of opportunity." As a historian, you know this is not the full picture. Write a response that compares the experience of two different groups in colonial America — showing how colonial life was NOT equal opportunity for everyone. Use specific evidence from what you have learned.

Examples of groups to compare: wealthy landowners vs. enslaved Africans · Puritan settlers vs. indentured servants · New England colonists vs. Southern plantation workers · free colonists vs. enslaved people

Aim for at least 4 sentences: introduce both groups → evidence for each → reasoned claim

Part 4 Virtue Lens Reflection
Service

Service means using your knowledge and skills to benefit others — including people who are not present to speak for themselves. Historians perform a kind of service when they honestly document the experiences of enslaved people, indentured servants, and others whose voices were silenced or left out of the official record. Without this work, future generations would only know part of the truth about colonial America. Think about what you learned in Unit 7. Give one specific example of how a historian serves justice by including a difficult or overlooked perspective in the historical record. Explain why that act of inclusion matters.
