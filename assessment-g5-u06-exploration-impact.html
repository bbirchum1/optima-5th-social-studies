Part 2 Source Task
Primary Source · Log of Christopher Columbus
Adapted from the journal of Christopher Columbus, October 1492, describing his first encounter with the Taíno people of the Caribbean
"They are well-built people of handsome stature. They bear no arms, nor know thereof. They are very gentle and do not know what evil is. I believe that they would become Christians very easily, for it seems to me that they had no religion. With fifty men we could subjugate them all and make them do whatever we wish."

Read the source carefully, then answer each question using evidence from the text.

A
What are two things Columbus says about the Taíno people in this source? Quote directly from the text to support each point.
B
Columbus says "I believe they had no religion." What problem does a historian have with this claim? What does it tell us about Columbus's perspective?
C
By the end of this source, Columbus mentions "making them do whatever we wish." What does this suggest about his real motives? How does this change how we should interpret his earlier, seemingly positive comments about the Taíno?
Part 3 Explorer Impact Claim

The Hasty Herald claims: "European exploration of the Americas was a great achievement that brought civilization and progress to the New World." As a historian, you know the full story is more complicated. Write a response that presents a more complete and honest account of the impact of European exploration. Include both positive and negative consequences, use specific evidence, and make a claim about the most important thing we must understand about this period in history.

Aim for at least 4 sentences: balanced account → both sides of evidence → reasoned claim

Part 4 Virtue Lens Reflection
Courage + Responsibility

Courage in history doesn't just mean sailing into the unknown — it also means being brave enough to tell the complete story, even when it's uncomfortable. Historians who study the Age of Exploration must have the courage to include difficult truths: the destruction of indigenous civilizations, the spread of devastating diseases, and the beginning of the Atlantic slave trade. Responsibility means recognizing the effects our actions have on others. Think about what you learned in Unit 6. In what way does studying this unit require courage as a learner? Give one specific example of a difficult truth you had to face — and explain why it matters to include it in the historical record.
