Part 1 Knowledge Check Fill-Blanks — Answer Key
1
The largest Mound Builder city, __________, had an estimated population of 10,000–20,000 at its peak — larger than London at the same time period.
✓ Correct Answer

Cahokia

2
The Aztec capital __________ was an island city with aqueducts, floating gardens (chinampas), and over 200,000 residents.
✓ Correct Answer

Tenochtitlan

3
The __________ Confederacy was an Eastern Woodlands peoples who governed six nations under a democratic Great Law of Peace, partly led by clan mothers.
✓ Correct Answer

Haudenosaunee

Accept "Iroquois" but note that "Haudenosaunee" is the preferred name used by the confederacy itself.

4
The __________ governed an empire of ~12 million people across 2,500 miles of South American mountains, connecting it all with roads and knotted-string records called __________.
✓ Correct Answers (2 blanks)

Blank 1: Inca

Blank 2: quipu

Both blanks must be correct for full 2 pts. Award 1 pt if only one blank is correct.

5
The term __________ refers to the time period BEFORE Christopher Columbus arrived in the Americas in 1492.
✓ Correct Answer

pre-Columbian

Part 2 Source Task — Answer Key
Source A — Spanish Explorer's Account, 1519
"When we arrived at the great city, I could not speak, for I had never seen anything so magnificent. The streets were wide, and canals ran alongside them. Markets were everywhere, filled with gold, cloth, and food of every kind. It was larger than any city in Spain."
Source B — American Textbook, 1952
"Before Columbus arrived, North America was a vast, mostly empty wilderness. A few small tribes of hunters and gatherers roamed the land."
2A
Which source gives more reliable evidence about the scale and complexity of pre-Columbian civilizations? Explain with specific evidence.
✓ Suggested Answer

Source A is more reliable for this question.

Source A provides specific, firsthand details: wide streets, canals, large markets with gold and cloth, and a city larger than any in Spain. These details give concrete evidence of a complex, large-scale urban civilization. The author was a firsthand witness — he was present in the city himself.

Source B makes a sweeping claim ("mostly empty wilderness") with no specific evidence to support it, and contradicts what archaeologists and historians now know about pre-Columbian civilizations.

Award full credit for identifying Source A as more reliable AND citing at least one specific detail from Source A as evidence. Deduct credit if student says Source B without strong reasoning.

2B
What important information does Source B get wrong or leave out? Use specific evidence from Units 3 and 4.
✓ Suggested Answer

Source B falsely claims North America was "mostly empty wilderness" with only "small tribes of hunters and gatherers." This is inaccurate. Evidence from Units 3 and 4 shows:

• Unit 3: Tenochtitlan (Aztec) had 200,000+ residents; Cahokia (Mound Builders) had 10,000–20,000; the Maya developed a writing system, calendar, and observatories; the Inca governed 12 million people.

• Unit 4: The Haudenosaunee formed a democratic confederacy of six nations; Pacific Northwest tribes had complex economies based on salmon trade; Desert Southwest Pueblo peoples built multi-story adobe cities; Great Plains tribes had sophisticated knowledge of buffalo ecology and migration.

Award full credit for identifying the specific inaccuracy (claiming "empty wilderness") AND citing at least two pieces of evidence from Units 3 or 4. Strong responses draw from both units.

2C
What questions should an honest editor ask about Source A before using it?
✓ Suggested Answer

Strong questions an honest editor should ask:

1. Whose perspective is this? The author is a Spanish conqueror — he came to take over the city, not just describe it. His account may be shaped by a desire to show how impressive a conquest it was.

2. What is missing? The Aztec people's own perspective on this encounter is not included. What did the Aztec people think when the Spanish arrived? Their voices are absent.

3. What was the author's purpose? Was he writing to report honestly, or to impress someone? If this was written for Spanish leaders, he may have exaggerated or framed things to serve that audience.

Award full credit for identifying the author's biased perspective (Spanish conqueror) AND naming at least one thing missing or potentially distorted. Partial credit for noting it's a primary source without analyzing the perspective.

Part 3 Historian's Claim — Scoring Guide
Correct the Hasty Herald's headline ("Before Europeans Arrived, North America Was Mostly Empty Wilderness With Simple Communities") using specific evidence from Units 3 AND 4. Include at least THREE pieces of evidence, a clear claim, and your reasoning.
Scoring Notes — Synthesis Claim
Secure (10–12 pts)
Student makes a clear counter-claim (something like: "North America before colonization was home to diverse, sophisticated civilizations with complex governments, cities, economies, and cultures") AND uses at least three specific pieces of evidence drawn from both Unit 3 AND Unit 4. Evidence is specific — names cities, civilizations, cultural practices, population figures, or structures. Reasoning connects evidence to the claim: explains WHY these examples disprove "empty wilderness." Draws from both units (not just one).
Developing (6–9 pts)
Student makes a counter-claim and includes evidence but draws from only one unit, OR includes three examples but they are general rather than specific (e.g., "the Aztec had a big city" without naming Tenochtitlan or its features). Reasoning is present but underdeveloped.
Beginning (4–5 pts)
Student disagrees with the headline but provides only one or two vague examples. No clear claim. Evidence is not specific enough to support the argument.

Look for strong evidence combinations like: Tenochtitlan (200k+ population, island city, aqueducts) + Cahokia (10–20k population, larger than London) + Haudenosaunee democratic confederacy + Pacific Northwest potlatch economy + Great Plains buffalo knowledge. Any 3+ pieces drawn from both units earns full evidence credit.

Part 4 Virtue Lens — Scoring Guide
Honesty + Courtesy: What is one important truth about pre-colonization North America that an honest historian must include? Why does leaving it out show a lack of honesty or courtesy?
Scoring Notes — Virtue Lens Reflection
Secure (3 pts)
Student names a specific truth (e.g., the sophistication and scale of pre-Columbian cities; the democratic governance structures of the Haudenosaunee; the diversity of cultures across regions) AND explains why omitting it is dishonest or discourteous. Strong examples: "An honest historian must include that Tenochtitlan was one of the largest cities in the world at the time — leaving this out creates a false impression that Indigenous peoples had simple societies, which is both inaccurate and disrespectful to their descendants." OR "Leaving out the Haudenosaunee democratic confederacy is dishonest because it erases a major political achievement that influenced later governments."
Developing (2 pts)
Student names a truth from the unit but explanation of WHY omitting it is dishonest/discourteous is general rather than specific.
Beginning (1 pt)
Student says something true about pre-Columbian peoples but doesn't connect it to honesty or courtesy as virtues practiced by historians.
