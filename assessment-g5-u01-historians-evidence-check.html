Before You Begin — How This Assessment Works

This assessment has 4 parts. Read each prompt carefully before you begin writing. Use your Historian's Notebook to plan longer responses before typing.

Part 1 — Knowledge Check: use the Word Bank to fill in the blanks. Part 2 — Source Task: study a primary source and answer guided questions. Part 3 — Historian's Claim: write a full constructed response. Part 4 — Virtue Lens: reflect on honesty in historical work.

When all 4 parts are complete, click Copy All Answers at the bottom and paste into your Canvas submission box.

Part 2 Source Task
Primary Source · Letter
Excerpt from a letter written by a Continental soldier at Valley Forge, December 1777
"We have been without proper clothing for weeks. Last night we had nothing to eat but firecake and water. Still, we remain. Our cause is just."

Read the source carefully, then answer each question using evidence from the text itself.

A
Is this a primary source or a secondary source? Give TWO pieces of evidence from the source itself to support your answer.
B
What does this source tell us about conditions at Valley Forge? Write one specific detail you can observe and one inference you can make.
C
What important information might this source NOT show? Whose perspective is missing?
Part 3 Historian's Claim

"Ms. Avery always says: 'An honest historian never relies on just one source.' Using what you learned in Unit 1, explain why historians need more than one kind of source to tell a complete story. Make a clear claim, use evidence from the lessons, and explain your reasoning."

Aim for at least 4 sentences: claim → evidence → reasoning → conclusion

Part 4 Virtue Lens Reflection
Honesty

Honesty means telling the truth, even when it's hard. What does honesty look like in the work of a historian? Give one specific example of how an honest historian uses primary and secondary sources together to tell a complete, fair story.
